Receipts in email, invoices as PDFs, totals in a web dashboard — the
numbers you need at tax time are scattered across screens. This workflow
turns each of them into one searchable, extractable capture.

## The walkthrough

1. **Capture as you go.** Whenever a receipt, invoice, or confirmation
   crosses your screen, **⌘⇧C** it. Long statements:
   [scrolling capture](/docs/guide/capture/#scrolling-capture) (**⌘⇧W**)
   stitches the whole page.

2. **File it in a collection.** Right-click → add to a
   [collection](/docs/guide/library/#collections-and-favorites) like
   *Receipts 2026*, and add a tag or two. Sealshot's automatic Smart
   Keywords and OCR handle the rest of the findability.

3. **Extract the data when you need it.** Open a capture and hit
   [**Extract Structured Data**](/docs/guide/ai/#extract-structured-data)
   — tables, line items, totals, dates, and vendor fields come out as
   clean, copyable text, ready to paste into your spreadsheet or expense
   tool. The result is cached in the `.seal`, so re-opening it is instant.

   ![The Extracted Data window pulling an invoice apart](/manual/extract-data.png)

4. **Find any amount later.** Search
   [looks inside the images](/docs/guide/library/#search) — a vendor name,
   an invoice number, even an amount. On Apple Intelligence Macs the query
   expands to related terms ("invoice" also finds "receipt").

5. **Redact before forwarding.** Sending a receipt to someone else? A
   quick [Smart Redact](/docs/guide/redaction/) pass covers the card
   number and address first.

:::tip
Everything here — OCR, extraction, keywords, search — runs
[on your Mac](/docs/guide/ai/). Your financial paperwork never touches a
cloud service, which is rather the point.
:::
