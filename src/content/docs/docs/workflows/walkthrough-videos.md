---
title: "Workflow: record a walkthrough people can follow"
description: Narrated screen recordings with clean audio, pauses between steps, and a shareable video file.
---

"Click here, then here" emails don't work. A ninety-second narrated
recording does. This workflow produces a walkthrough video with clean
voice audio and no dead air — without a video editor.

## The walkthrough

1. **Set the stage.** Close what shouldn't be on screen (or plan to record
   just a window). Press **⌘⇧R** and pick the window or region — or
   **⌘⇧V** for the [whole screen](/docs/guide/recording/).

2. **Check the Record prompt.** Turn **microphone** on for narration —
   **Reduce microphone noise** (on by default) suppresses background hum
   and levels your voice, processed on your Mac. Keep the **3-second
   countdown** so you can breathe before speaking.

   ![The Record prompt](/manual/record-prompt.png)

3. **Pause between steps.** When you need to set up the next part, hit
   **⌘⇧P** — pausing from the keyboard keeps the mouse trip to the HUD out
   of your recording. Press it again to resume; the
   [HUD](/docs/guide/recording/#while-recording) shows Recording/Paused
   status the whole time.

4. **Stop and review.** The recording lands in your
   [Library](/docs/guide/library/) as a video `.seal` and plays right in
   the editor — press **Space** in the Library for a quick look.

5. **Export and send.** **File → Export to Video…** writes a standard
   `.mp4`/`.mov` anyone can play. Sending something sensitive? Package it
   encrypted instead — see
   [sharing sensitive documents](/docs/workflows/sensitive-documents/).

:::tip
Pair the video with two or three annotated stills for the steps people
will want to reference later — captures and recordings live side by side
in the Library, so grab frames of the key moments and
[annotate](/docs/guide/editor/) them.
:::
